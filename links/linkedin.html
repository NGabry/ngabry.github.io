      About

      I am a late-stage PhD candidate in the Biology Department at Indiana State University with a focus in avian evolutionary ecology, data science, and bioinformatics. My current project involves the computational analysis of next-generation sequencing (NGS) outputs generated from eDNA to determine diet and resource disparities in a polymorphic sparrow. Additionally, I am working to establish a behavioral paradigm in a laboratory setting for the future research of promiscuity and monogamy in birds. As a research assistant for The Center of Genomic Advocacy, I received professional training in NGS library preparation and sequencing, and am a self taught full-stack data scientist utilizing various UNIX based tools for big-data analysis. I have six years of experience in research lab environments, combined with four years of experience working in the field with avian models. Extremely comfortable and capable as a project lead both in the field, and in the lab.
